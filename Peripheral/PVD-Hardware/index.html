Peripheral Vascular Intervention Masterclass

Complete Guide to Access, Wires, Balloons, Stents, CTO Techniques & Advanced Devices

1. Endovascular Treatment Workflow

Every successful peripheral intervention follows the same pathway:

  1. Access the artery
  2. Cross the lesion
  3. Create support
  4. Prepare the vessel
  5. Treat the lesion
  6. Manage complications

2. Access Techniques

Contralateral Femoral Access

Most common approach. Easy puncture and crossover technique.

Ipsilateral Antegrade Access

Provides maximum pushability for SFA and BTK interventions.

Retrograde Tibial Access

Useful when antegrade crossing fails.

3. Guidewires

Fielder FC

  • Hydrophilic
  • Soft tip
  • Excellent for stenotic lesions
  • Tibial interventions

Command Wire

  • Workhorse wire
  • Excellent torque
  • Soft CTO crossing

CTO Wire Escalation

Lesion Wire
Soft CTO Command
Fibrotic CTO Halberd
Calcified CTO Astato XS20
Extreme Calcium Astato XS40 / Winn

4. Support Catheters

CXI Support Catheter

Improves support, pushability and wire exchange.

Progreat Microcatheter

Excellent for BTK interventions and subintimal tracking.

5. Balloon Angioplasty

Balloon Sizing Principles

  • Diameter = vessel diameter
  • Length = lesion + margin
  • Predilation before stenting

6. Special Balloons

Drug Coated Balloon

Paclitaxel or Sirolimus coated balloons.

Chocolate Balloon

Controlled expansion and reduced dissection.

Cutting Balloon

Ideal for resistant fibrotic lesions.

Shockwave IVL

Fractures calcium before angioplasty.

7. Peripheral Stents

Self Expanding Nitinol Stents

  • Zilver Flex
  • Absolute Pro
  • Misago
  • Innova

Supera Stent

The most important peripheral stent for examination purposes.
  • Interwoven nitinol design
  • Exceptional flexibility
  • Excellent crush resistance
  • Very low fracture rate
  • Ideal for distal SFA and popliteal artery
